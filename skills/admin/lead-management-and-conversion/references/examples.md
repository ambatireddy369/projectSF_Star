# Examples — Lead Management and Conversion

## Example 1: Web-to-Lead Form with reCAPTCHA and Custom Field Capture

**Context:** A B2B company runs a "Request a Demo" page on their marketing site. They expect 200–400 leads per day during campaign launches and want to prevent bots from exhausting the 500/day limit.

**Problem:** The default web-to-lead form has no spam protection. During a paid search campaign, bot traffic submitted over 500 requests in 4 hours, blocking all legitimate inbound leads for the rest of the day. Sales had no visibility into the block until a prospect called to say their form submission was rejected.

**Solution:**

```html
<!-- Generated by Salesforce Web-to-Lead with reCAPTCHA enabled -->
<form action="https://webto.salesforce.com/servlet/servlet.WebToLead?encoding=UTF-8" method="POST">
  <input type="hidden" name="oid" value="00D000000000000">
  <!-- Return to a branded thank-you page, not salesforce.com -->
  <input type="hidden" name="retURL" value="https://www.example.com/thank-you">
  <!-- reCAPTCHA token field is injected by the Google reCAPTCHA JS widget -->
  <input type="hidden" name="g-recaptcha-response" id="g-recaptcha-response">

  <label for="first_name">First Name</label>
  <input id="first_name" maxlength="40" name="first_name" type="text" required />

  <label for="last_name">Last Name</label>
  <input id="last_name" maxlength="80" name="last_name" type="text" required />

  <label for="email">Email</label>
  <input id="email" maxlength="80" name="email" type="email" required />

  <label for="company">Company</label>
  <input id="company" maxlength="40" name="company" type="text" required />

  <!-- Custom field: maps to Lead.Product_Interest__c (API name) -->
  <label for="00N000000000001">Product Interest</label>
  <select id="00N000000000001" name="00N000000000001">
    <option value="">--Select--</option>
    <option value="Sales Cloud">Sales Cloud</option>
    <option value="Service Cloud">Service Cloud</option>
  </select>

  <div class="g-recaptcha" data-sitekey="YOUR_RECAPTCHA_SITE_KEY"></div>
  <input type="submit" value="Request Demo">
</form>
<script src="https://www.google.com/recaptcha/api.js" async defer></script>
```

**Why it works:** The reCAPTCHA widget challenges non-human traffic before the form POSTs to Salesforce. Automated submissions without a valid `g-recaptcha-response` token are rejected by Salesforce at intake. Legitimate submissions continue to consume the 500/day quota normally. The `retURL` points to a company-branded page so users see a clean confirmation experience.

## Example 2: Custom Field Mapping for Lead Conversion

**Context:** A SaaS company uses a custom Lead field `Demo_Requested__c` (Checkbox) and `Lead_Score__c` (Number) to qualify inbound leads. Sales reps convert qualified leads manually. After conversion, sales ops discovered that the Contact and Opportunity records had no trace of the demo request flag or lead score — reps had no context for their first call.

**Problem:** Both custom fields were not mapped in Lead Field Mapping. Conversion silently dropped both values. There was no error during conversion — the process completed successfully and the data was lost.

**Solution:**

Steps to fix:

1. Object Manager > Lead > Fields & Relationships > `Demo_Requested__c` > "Map Lead Field"
   - Contact target: `Demo_Requested__c` (create if it does not exist; must be Checkbox type)
   - Opportunity target: `Demo_Requested__c` (create if needed)
2. Object Manager > Lead > Fields & Relationships > `Lead_Score__c` > "Map Lead Field"
   - Contact target: `Lead_Score_at_Conversion__c` (Number — maps the score at time of conversion for reference)
   - Opportunity target: `Lead_Score_at_Conversion__c` (same field on Opportunity)
3. Convert a test Lead in a sandbox. Verify both fields appear on the resulting Contact and Opportunity with the correct values.
4. For historical records already converted with missing data: the original Lead record remains in the org with `IsConverted = true`. The values can be backfilled via Data Loader by querying converted Leads and updating the resulting Contact/Opportunity records.

```sql
-- SOQL to find converted leads with missing target data (run in Developer Console)
SELECT Id, Demo_Requested__c, Lead_Score__c, ConvertedContactId, ConvertedOpportunityId
FROM Lead
WHERE IsConverted = true
AND ConvertedDate = LAST_N_DAYS:30
AND Demo_Requested__c = true
```

**Why it works:** Once the mapping is configured, all future conversions transfer the field values. The mapping is stored in org metadata (`LeadConvertSettings`) and is included in change sets and unlocked packages.

## Example 3: Auto-Response Rule That Actually Fires

**Context:** A nonprofit uses Web-to-Lead to capture volunteer interest. They configured an auto-response rule to send a "Thank you for your interest" email immediately on submission, but the email was never delivered.

**Problem:** The auto-response rule criteria were correct and the email template existed, but there was no active assignment rule in the org. The nonprofit had skipped assignment rule setup because all leads go to one coordinator. Without an active assignment rule firing, the auto-response rule is silently skipped — this is by design in Salesforce's rule evaluation order.

**Solution:**

1. Setup > Lead Assignment Rules > New. Name: "Default All Leads Rule". Mark Active.
2. Add one rule entry with no criteria (catch-all). Assign to the coordinator's user record.
3. Set email template on the rule entry to the coordinator notification template.
4. Separately, confirm the auto-response rule exists: Setup > Lead Auto-Response Rules. Activate the rule. Confirm at least one rule entry has criteria (or a catch-all) and an email template.
5. Submit a test lead via the web form. Confirm:
   - The lead is created and owned by the coordinator.
   - The submitter receives the auto-response email.
   - The coordinator receives the assignment notification.

**Why it works:** The assignment rule must fire on the same record as the auto-response rule for the response email to be sent. Once a catch-all assignment rule entry exists, every web-to-lead submission triggers both the assignment and the auto-response evaluation.

## Anti-Pattern: Assuming Conversion Preserves All Fields Without Checking

**What practitioners do:** An admin creates a dozen custom Lead fields for qualification data — BANT scores, competitor mentions, product interest tags — and converts leads without verifying field mapping. They assume Salesforce moves all data on conversion.

**What goes wrong:** All custom fields that were not explicitly mapped in Object Manager > Lead > Map Lead Fields are silently dropped. The Contact and Opportunity records exist with no qualification context. Sales reps lose the research their SDRs captured. Post-conversion the data exists only on the original (now converted) Lead record.

**Correct approach:** Before deploying any custom Lead fields to production:
1. Document each custom field and its intended post-conversion home (Contact, Account, or Opportunity).
2. Create matching fields on the target objects with matching data types.
3. Map each field in Object Manager > Lead > Fields & Relationships > Map Lead Fields.
4. Test conversion in a sandbox and inspect all three resulting records.
5. Include field mapping audit in every change set review for Lead field additions.
